Fun fact, a restaurant can have rats found during an inspection and still receive a passing grade.

City health department grading systems are designed for enforcement, not for consumers like us. 2. What We Collect

We only collect data that serves a clear purpose. Here's everything, broken down by category:

2.1 Account Information

What: Email address
When: When you create an account (via email/password, Apple Sign In, or Google Sign In)
Why: To authenticate you and associate your preferences with your account
Notes: If you use Apple Sign In with email hiding, we only receive your relay address — we never see your real email.

2.2 Location Data

What: Your approximate geographic location (latitude/longitude)
When: Only while the App is open and only if you grant location permission
Why: To show you nearby restaurants and their inspection results on the map

  • Location permission is entirely optional. You can decline it and instead select a metro area manually.
  • We request "When In Use" permission only — we never request background or "Always On" location access.
  • Your location is used for real-time queries and is not stored in a location history or profile.

2.3 Your Saved Data

What:

  • Pinned establishments — restaurants you save, including optional custom nicknames you assign
  • Notification preferences — whether you want alerts when pinned establishments receive new inspections
  • Sensitivity settings — your customized violation severity preferences (e.g., which violation types matter most to you)
  • Push notification tokens — a device-specific token generated by Apple that allows us to deliver notifications to your device

When: When you pin a restaurant, toggle notification preferences, adjust your sensitivity settings, or enable push notifications
Why: To personalize the App to your priorities, restore your settings across sessions, and deliver inspection update notifications
Notes: Your pins, preferences, and sensitivity settings are private to your account. They are never shared with other users, establishments, or third parties. Push notification tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM before storage. The token is specific to this app — it cannot be used to track you across other apps or identify your device. We store a one-way hash (SHA-256) of the token for deduplication; the original token is only decrypted in memory when sending a notification.

2.4 Usage Data

What: We track three types of in-app events:

  • Establishment views — which restaurant you viewed (establishment ID, name, city)
  • Inspection views — which inspection you viewed (establishment ID, inspection ID, name)
  • Search queries — what you searched for, how many results were returned, and which city

When: As you browse and search within the App
Why: To understand which features are used, improve search quality, and identify data gaps (e.g., a city where users frequently search but we have poor coverage)

  • Usage data is tied to your authenticated account (user ID) — it is not collected anonymously.
  • We do not track: screens visited, time spent, scroll behavior, tap heatmaps, or any interactions outside the three events listed above.
  • Usage tracking is completely disabled in development/debug builds.

2.5 Form Submissions

What: Content you voluntarily submit through in-app forms and the Website:

  • Feedback — free-text message
  • Feature requests — title and optional description
  • City requests — city name, optional email address, optional phone number
  • Support requests — category (bug report, feature request, general question, or account issue), email address, and message (submitted via the App's Settings or the Website's support page)

When: When you choose to submit a form
Why: To hear from you, respond to issues, and improve the Service

  • Email and phone on city request forms are optional and only used if we need to follow up with you about your request.
  • Support requests require an email address so we can respond to you.
  • City request forms can be submitted during onboarding before you have an account — in that case, no user ID is attached.
  • Support requests submitted via the Website are not linked to your app account.
  • All form inputs are sanitized server-side to protect against malicious content.

2.6 Device Information

What: Basic device metadata (device model, OS version, app version)
When: Submitted alongside form submissions only
Why: To help us reproduce and debug issues reported via feedback forms
Notes: We do not collect device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV), hardware serial numbers, or carrier information. For device integrity data (App Attest), see Section 2.7.

2.7 Push Notification Tokens

What: A device-specific token issued by Apple's Push Notification service (APNs) that identifies your device for notification delivery
When: When you enable push notifications in the App
Why: To deliver alerts when your pinned establishments receive new health inspections, and to notify you about important subscription events (e.g., trial expiration)

  • Push notification tokens are encrypted at rest on our servers using AES-256-GCM encryption.
  • Tokens are retained while your account is active. Invalidated tokens are cleaned up within 90 days.
  • You can disable push notifications at any time through your device's iOS Settings.
  • We do not use push notifications for marketing or advertising.

2.8 Device Integrity Verification

What: We use Apple's App Attest service to verify that requests to our servers come from a genuine, unmodified copy of the Sanity app. When your device is attested, we store:

  • A device-specific public key (generated by Apple's secure hardware)
  • A sign count (a counter used to prevent replay attacks)
  • An environment indicator (production or development)
  • A timestamp of the last verification

When: When you first use the App and periodically during normal use
Why: To prevent unauthorized access to our servers and protect our proprietary data from scraping and abuse

  • App Attest is a privacy-preserving technology designed by Apple. The stored key is unique to this app installation — it cannot be used to identify your device, track you across apps, or link to your hardware.
  • We do not use App Attest data for analytics, advertising, or profiling.
  • This data is not linked to your user account. It is associated with your app installation, not your identity.

2.9 What We Do NOT Collect

To be explicit:

  • No contacts or address book
  • No photos, camera, or microphone access
  • No health data from HealthKit
  • No browsing history or cross-app tracking
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA)
  • No device fingerprinting
  • No cookies (neither the App nor the Website uses cookies)
  • No IP address logging for analytics purposes — however, IP addresses are temporarily logged for rate limiting and abuse prevention on our Website and public API endpoints (see Section 2.9)
  • No biometric data

2.10 Rate Limiting Data

What: IP addresses and the name of the endpoint accessed
When: When you submit forms on our Website or access public API endpoints
Why: To enforce rate limits and prevent abuse (e.g., spam submissions)

  • Rate limit logs are automatically cleaned as part of each rate limit check. Entries older than the rate limit window (typically under 5 minutes) are deleted.
  • We do not use IP addresses for analytics, tracking, identification, or any purpose other than rate limiting.
  • IP addresses are not linked to your user account.

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 Where Your Data Lives

Your data is stored on Supabase infrastructure in the US East (us-east-1) region, hosted on AWS.

5.2 How We Protect It

  • Encryption in transit: All connections use TLS (HTTPS). Data between your device and our servers is always encrypted.
  • Encryption at rest: Database storage is encrypted at rest via Supabase/AWS infrastructure.
  • Device token encryption: Push notification tokens are individually encrypted using AES-256-GCM before storage. Tokens are only decrypted in memory at the moment a notification is sent.
  • Row-Level Security (RLS): Database policies ensure users can only access their own data. Your pins, preferences, events, and account information are isolated from other users at the database level.
  • Authentication: Secure JWT-based authentication with token expiration and refresh.
  • Access control: The website uses Supabase anonymous keys for public form submissions. Service-level keys with elevated privileges are never exposed to client applications. Administrative access is restricted.
  • Rate limiting: API endpoints are rate-limited to prevent abuse. Limits vary by endpoint and are enforced per-device, per-user, or per-IP address depending on the authentication method. Rate limit data is automatically cleaned (see Section 2.9).

5.3 What We Can't Guarantee

No system is 100% secure. While we use industry-standard protections, we cannot guarantee absolute security against all threats. If we become aware of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you in accordance with applicable law.
