About Sanity

City health department grading systems are designed for enforcement, not for consumers. A restaurant can have rats found during an inspection and still receive a passing grade.

Sanity re-evaluates the same raw inspection data through a consumer lens, applying its own deterministic severity rankings so you see what actually matters to you.

FAQ

What is Sanity?

Sanity is a consumer-focused health inspection app. We take the same raw inspection data published by city health departments and re-evaluate it through a lens that actually matters to diners.

How is Sanity different from a city grade?

City grading systems are designed for enforcement, not for you. A restaurant can have rodents found during an inspection and still pass. Sanity applies its own severity rankings so you see what's actually important.

1. Who We Are

Sanity ("we," "us," "our") operates the Sanity mobile application (the "App"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use the App.

2. What We Collect

We only collect data that serves a clear purpose. Here's everything, broken down by category:

2.1 Account Information

What: Email address
When: When you create an account (via email/password, Apple Sign In, Google Sign In, or Facebook Login)
Why: To authenticate you and associate your preferences with your account
Notes: If you use Apple Sign In with email hiding, we only receive your relay address — we never see your real email.

2.2 Location Data

What: Your approximate geographic location (latitude/longitude)
When: Only while the App is open and only if you grant location permission
Why: To show you nearby restaurants and their inspection results on the map

  • Location permission is entirely optional. You can decline it and instead select a metro area manually.
  • We request "When In Use" permission only — we never request background or "Always On" location access.
  • Your location is used for real-time queries and is not stored in a location history or profile.

2.3 Your Saved Data

What:

  • Pinned establishments — restaurants you save, including optional custom nicknames you assign
  • Notification preferences — whether you want alerts when pinned establishments receive new inspections

When: When you pin a restaurant or toggle notification preferences
Why: To personalize the App to your priorities and restore your settings across sessions
Notes: Your pins and preferences are private to your account. They are never shared with other users, establishments, or third parties.

2.4 Usage Data

What: We track three types of in-app events:

  • Establishment views — which restaurant you viewed (establishment ID, name, city)
  • Inspection views — which inspection you viewed (establishment ID, inspection ID, name)
  • Search queries — what you searched for, how many results were returned, and which city

When: As you browse and search within the App
Why: To understand which features are used, improve search quality, and identify data gaps (e.g., a city where users frequently search but we have poor coverage)

  • Usage data is tied to your authenticated account (user ID) — it is not collected anonymously.
  • We do not track: screens visited, time spent, scroll behavior, tap heatmaps, or any interactions outside the three events listed above.
  • Usage tracking is completely disabled in development/debug builds.

2.5 Form Submissions

What: Content you voluntarily submit through in-app forms:

  • Feedback — free-text message
  • Feature requests — title and optional description
  • City requests — city name, optional email address, optional phone number

When: When you choose to submit a form
Why: To hear from you and improve the App

  • Email and phone on city request forms are optional and only used if we need to follow up with you about your request.
  • City request forms can be submitted during onboarding before you have an account — in that case, no user ID is attached.

2.6 Device Information

What: Basic device metadata (device model, OS version, app version)
When: Submitted alongside form submissions only
Why: To help us reproduce and debug issues reported via feedback forms
Notes: We do not collect device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV), hardware serial numbers, or carrier information.

2.7 What We Do NOT Collect

To be explicit:

  • No contacts or address book
  • No photos, camera, or microphone access
  • No health data from HealthKit
  • No browsing history or cross-app tracking
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA)
  • No device fingerprinting
  • No cookies (we're a native app, not a website)
  • No IP address logging for analytics purposes
  • No biometric data

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 Where Your Data Lives

Your data is stored on Supabase infrastructure in the US East (us-east-1) region, hosted on AWS.

5.2 How We Protect It

  • Encryption in transit: All connections use TLS (HTTPS). Data between your device and our servers is always encrypted.
  • Encryption at rest: Database storage is encrypted at rest via Supabase/AWS infrastructure.
  • Row-Level Security (RLS): Database policies ensure users can only access their own data. Your pins, preferences, events, and account information are isolated from other users at the database level.
  • Authentication: Secure JWT-based authentication with token expiration and refresh.
  • Access control: Service-level keys are never exposed to client applications. Administrative access is restricted.
  • Rate limiting: Form submissions are rate-limited to prevent abuse (5 per 5-minute window).

5.3 What We Can't Guarantee

No system is 100% secure. While we use industry-standard protections, we cannot guarantee absolute security against all threats. If we become aware of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you in accordance with applicable law.

Sanity displays health inspection data for restaurants and food establishments. We take publicly available inspection records from city health departments and present them through a consumer-focused lens — highlighting what matters most to you as a diner.

Important things to understand:

  • We are not a health authority. We do not conduct inspections, issue grades, or certify establishments. All underlying data comes from official city health department records.
  • Our ratings are our interpretation. Sanity applies its own severity classifications to public violation data. Our health status indicators (red, orange, yellow, green) reflect our consumer-risk assessment, not the city's official grade. A restaurant with a passing city grade may show warnings in Sanity, and vice versa.
  • Data may be delayed or incomplete. Inspection records depend on when cities publish their data. There may be a lag between an inspection occurring and it appearing in the App. Not all violations in all cities have the same level of detail.
  • We are not a substitute for your judgment. Sanity is an informational tool. We do not guarantee the safety, quality, or hygiene of any establishment. Always use your own judgment when deciding where to eat.
